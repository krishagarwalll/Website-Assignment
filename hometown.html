Jaipur

About my hometown

My hometown, Jaipur, is full of great food, architecture, markets, and culture. Famously known as The Pink City of India, one of my favorite spots in the city is the Sheesh Mahal, a stunning palace adorned with thousands of tiny mirrors that shimmer like stars when light hits them. Then there’s Jal Mahal, a beautiful palace floating peacefully in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, creating a perfect reflection on the water. Located in the city’s center is the iconic Hawa Mahal, a pink palace with its unique honeycomb design and countless little windows that once allowed royal women to watch the street festivals while remaining hidden.

About the Culture

Culturally too, Jaipur is bustling with bazaars that are full of life and color. Johari Bazaar is famous for its stunning jewelry, from traditional Kundan pieces to beautiful gemstones—it's a paradise for anyone who loves a bit of sparkle. Then there’s Bapu Bazaar, where you can find everything from vibrant textiles and bandhani sarees to quirky souvenirs. Walking through these markets, you're surrounded by the sounds of bargaining and the smells of street food, creating a sensory overload that feels uniquely Jaipur. My hometown is also a hub for art and theater, with Jawahar Kala Kendra being the center of it all. This cultural space regularly hosts exhibitions, performances, and workshops that showcase Jaipur’s creative spirit. Whether it’s the lively markets or the thriving arts scene, there’s always something happening in Jaipur that keeps the cultural energy alive.

About Rajasthani Cuisine

When it comes to food, nothing can beat Jaipur’s café and restaurant scene. Let me talk about a couple of my favorites. For a luxurious experience, Leela Palace’s Mohan Mahal is pure elegance. With its candle-lit ambiance and intricate detailing, dining there feels like stepping into royalty, and the traditional Rajasthani dishes are an absolute treat. On the other hand, Tapri Ashram is my go-to for a more casual vibe. It's the perfect spot for chai and snacks, with a rooftop view and cozy, laid-back atmosphere that makes it a favorite among locals for long, chill hangouts. Whether you’re in the mood for a lavish dinner or a relaxed café, Jaipur’s food scene has it all.
